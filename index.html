SABER: Sustainable Alcohol By-Product Enhancement for Regenerative soils - A global approach to waste valorisation in agriculture

Funded by:

Who are we?

We are the SABER project team, a multinational research consortium comprising six leading universities across the United Kingdom, Mexico, New Zealand, and Spain. Led by Prof. Laura Carter (University of Leeds) and Dr. Carlos Moreno-García (RGU, Aberdeen), our multidisciplinary group gathers experts in soil science, waste valorisation, artificial intelligence (AI), sustainable business models and scientific dissemination to the wider public. We are already supported by major industry stakeholders, including Grupo Trabanco (cider), Hawkes Bay (wine), and the Mexican National Chamber of the Tequila Industry.

What do we want to do?

Our goal is to transform alcohol industry waste—such as spent grains, distillery sludge, and tequila bagasse—from an expensive disposal burden into high-value organic soil amendments like biochar and compost using data-driven AI decision making techniques. We aim to bridge the gap between waste generation and sustainable agriculture by investigating the impact of these materials on soil health across diverse climates. Ultimately, we are developing scalable, practical solutions that foster a global circular economy.

What do we need from you?

We seek active collaboration from the alcohol industry to ensure our research remains aligned with real-world industrial needs. Specifically, we invite you to:

  • Join our industrial partner group to provide strategic guidance.
  • Share industry-specific insights, relevant data, or waste material samples for characterisation.
  • Participate in international workshops to co-design implementable reuse strategies and predictive tools.

What's the benefit for you?

Partnering with SABER offers several strategic advantages:

  • Economic Savings: Substantially reduce your waste disposal costs and potentially diversify revenue through new valorised products.
  • Regulatory Readiness: Gain science-based evidence to help meet increasingly strict global waste reduction targets and carbon-neutral goals.
  • Enhanced Reputation: Strengthen your brand’s sustainability credentials and global competitiveness by adopting validated, "green" production practices.
  • Expert Access: Leverage our cutting-edge research, including AI-driven predictive modelling and international networks, to solve your specific waste challenges.

What have we done so far?

We have successfully secured NERC (UK) and SECIHTI (Mexico) funding to establish a robust international consortium with research facilities in six universities across four countries, plus three industry partners. In November 2025, we conducted the AGAVE Symposium, an event that brought together students, academics and private sector stakeholders to solve environmental challenges in the state of Jalisco. Furthermore, we have validated our initial testing platforms, such as the soil microcosm framework, and secured formal commitments and "in-kind" resources from our founding industry partners.
